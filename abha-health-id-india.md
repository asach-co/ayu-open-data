# ABHA Health ID in India: A Digital Health Identifier

The Ayushman Bharat Health Account (ABHA) is a 14-digit unique identifier launched under India's Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission (ABDM). It aims to create a unified digital health infrastructure by allowing individuals to link and share their health records across ABDM-enrolled healthcare providers, with the goal of improving healthcare access and efficiency. ABHA number creation is voluntary and free, and can be linked to Aadhaar or a mobile number for verification.

## Key Features of the ABHA Number

The ABHA number functions as a unique health identifier for Indian citizens. It offers several core features:

*   **Unique Identification:** A 14-digit number assigned to each individual.
*   **Voluntary Participation:** Enrollment is not mandatory.
*   **Free of Charge:** No cost is associated with creating an ABHA number.
*   **Record Linking:** Allows linking of health records from various providers.
*   **Consent-Based Sharing:** Records are shared only with explicit consent.
*   **Not Aadhaar:** It is a separate health-specific ID, not the same as an Aadhaar number.

## Creating an ABHA Number

Several methods are available for creating an ABHA number:

*   **ABHA Website:** Through the official website (healthid.ndhm.gov.in).
*   **ABHA Mobile App:** Using the NHA mobile application.
*   **CoWIN:** Vaccination certificates may already be linked to an ABHA number if opted-in during registration.
*   **Facilitation Centres:** Available at government hospitals for those without Aadhaar-linked mobile numbers.

The process generally involves verifying identity via Aadhaar OTP or other methods and completing a basic profile.

## Information Linked to ABHA

With explicit consent, various types of health records can be linked to an ABHA account. These include:

*   OPD consultation notes and prescriptions.
*   Diagnostic reports (blood tests, imaging).
*   Discharge summaries.
*   Vaccination records.

The records are stored by individual health providers and linked to the ABHA account, not in a central government database. This distributed approach aims to ensure data privacy and security.

## Adoption and Limitations

While ABDM adoption is growing, limitations remain. As of 2026, over 500 million ABHA numbers have been created *(Source: NHA, 2026)*. Enrolled facilities include major hospital networks and diagnostic chains.

*   **Limited Enrollment:** Many small clinics and private practitioners are not yet ABDM-enrolled.
*   **Consent Process:** The consent process can be complex for some users.

Despite these limitations, ABHA aims to improve healthcare access and management for Indian citizens.
